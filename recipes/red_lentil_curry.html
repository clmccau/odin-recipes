Red Lentil Curry

This is a delicious vegan curry that can convince even a vegan skeptic to add it to their recipe rotation!

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp coconut or neutral flavored oil
  • 4 large cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inch piece of fresh ginger, peeled and minced or grated
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1-2 serrano peppers, diced (leaving seeds in will be spicier!)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp Indian red chili, or 1 tsp regular chili powder
  • 2 tsps curry powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp of salt (Kosher, sea, or pink)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup (180 - 190 g) red lentils, rinsed thoroughly and soaked for 1 - 2 hours
  • 2 cups (480 mL) low sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 (14 oz/400 g) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (13.5 oz/400 mL) can full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp unsweetened creamy almond butter
  • 1/2 a small lemon, juiced
  • 1/2 cup (8 g) fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • Basmati or Jasmine rice and your choice of Indian flatbread

Prep

Complete these steps first to make the actual cooking stress free.

  • While not necessary, soaking the lentils beforehand will improve the cooking time as well as digestion. 1 - 2 hours should be sufficient.
  • Prepare the garlic, ginger, and peppers. Set aside in a small bowl.
  • Combine the cumin, coriander, chili powder, curry powder, garam masala, salt, black pepper, and turmeric. Set aside in a small bowl.
  • Open your cans of tomatoes and coconut milk.
  • Have the two cups of vegetable broth in a wet measurement vessel.
  • Rinse 1 - 3 cups of rice and start cooking via rice cooker.

Instructions

  1. Heat a large, deep skillet or sauce pan over medium heat and add your oil. Once the oil is shimmering, add the garlic, ginger, and peppers. Cook for 2 minutes while stirring constantly so the garlic doesn't burn.
  2. Add the mix of spices and cook for another 30 to 60 seconds until fragrant, tossing frequently to prevent burning.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and deglaze the bottom of the pan with a wooden spatula. Add the tomatoes and lentils, then mix well.
  4. Set the temperature to a gentle simmer and cover. Cook for 20 to 25 minutes, stirring occasionally. The lentils should be mostly softened by the end. If not, simmer a few minutes longer. A small amount of broth may be added if needed.
  5. Remove the lid and stir in the coconut milk, almond butter, and any additional salt and pepper to taste. Continue cooking until the curry begins to thicken. (5 - 8 minutes)
  6. Finally, add the lemon juice and freshly chopped cilantro.
